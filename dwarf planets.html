Dwarf Planets

Pluto

Read time: 3 min

  1. Name Origin: The Roman god of the underworld.
  2. Size: With a radius of 715 miles (1,151 kilometers)
  3. Location: In the Kuiper Belt beyond Neptune’s orbit.
  4. Temperature: as cold as -375 to -400 degrees Fahrenheit (-226 to -240 degrees Celsius).
  5. Distance: 3.7 billion miles (5.9 billion kilometers).
  6. Orbit Duration: 248 Earth years.
  7. Day Duration: 153 hours.

Curious Facts:

  • It is the only planet named by an 11-year-old girl.
  • It has five known moons: Charon, Nix, Hydra, Kerberos, and Styx.
  • Pluto's orbit around the Sun is oval and its axis of rotation is tilted 57 degrees against it so it spins nearly sideways.Its oval orbit makes it switch places with Neptune for 20 years every 248 Earth years.
  • It has heart-shaped glacier.

Ceres

Read time: 3 min

  1. Name Origin: the Roman goddess of corn and harvests.
  2. Size: radius of 296 miles (476 kilometers)
  3. Location: in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.
  4. Distance: 257 million miles (413 million kilometers)
  5. Orbit Duration: 4.6 Earth years
  6. Day Duration: 9 hours.

Curious Facts:

  • It is the largest object in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.
  • It's the only dwarf planet located in the inner solar system.

Makemake

Read time: 3 min

  1. Name Origine: the Rapanui god of fertility.
  2. Size: radius of approximately 444 miles (715 kilometers)
  3. Location: In the Kuiper Belt beyond Neptune’s orbit.
  4. Distance: 4,253,000,000 miles (6,847,000,000 kilometers).
  5. Orbit Duration: 305.34 Earth years
  6. Day Duration: 22.48 hours

Curious Facts:

  • Makemake is slightly smaller than Pluto.
  • It is the second brightest object in the Kuiper Belt as seen from Earth while Pluto is the brightest.
  • Makemake has one provisional moon, nicknamed MK 2.

Haumea

Read time: 3 min

  1. Name Origin: the Hawaiian goddess of fertility.
  2. Size: a radius of about 385 miles (620 kilometers).
  3. Location: In the Kuiper Belt beyond Neptune’s orbit.
  4. Distance:4,010,000,000 miles (6,452,000,000 kilometers)
  5. Orbit Duration: 285 Earth years
  6. Day Duration: 4 hours.

Curious Facts:

  • It is one of the fastest rotating large objects in our solar system.
  • It has oval shape.
  • Haumea is the first known Kuiper Belt Object to have rings.
  • Haumea has two known moons: Namaka is the inner moon, and Hi'iaka is the outer moon

Eris

Read time: 3 min

  1. Name Origin: the ancient Greek goddess of discord and strife.
  2. Size: radius of about 722 miles (1,163 kilometers).
  3. Location: In the Kuiper Belt beyond Neptune’s orbit.
  4. Temperature: Varies from -359 degrees Fahrenheit (-217 degrees Celsius) to -405 degrees Fahrenheit (-243 degrees Celsius).
  5. Distance: 6,289,000,000 miles (10,125,000,000 kilometers).
  6. Orbit Duration: 557 Earth years.
  7. Day Duration: 25.9 hours.

Curious Facts:

  • One of the biggest known dwarf planets in the Solar System.
  • Its discovery led to debate and Pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet.
  • Eris has a very small moon called Dysnomia.
