Our Solar System in Facts

Read time: 3 min

  1. Formation: 4.6 billion years ago
  2. It has:
    • one star;
    • eight planets;
    • five officially recognised dwarf planets;
    • at least 290 moons;
    • more than 1.3 million asteroids;
    • about 3,900 comets.
  3. Location: Orion Spur - an outer spiral arm of the Milky Way galaxy.
  4. Speed: It orbits the center of the galaxy at about 515,000 mph (828,000 kph).
  5. Orbit Duration: about 230 million years.
  6. Explore more: Solar System View

Facts about the Sun

Read time: 3 min

  1. Name Origin:
    • Latin word “Sol” - for sun-related adjectives;
    • Helios (the ancient Greek God of the sun) - for sun-related terminology.
  2. Size: 435,000 miles (700,000 kilometers).
  3. Type: G2 V, yellow dwarf main-sequence star.
  4. Temperature:
    • The core: more than 27 million °F (15 million °C);
    • The photosphere - 10,000 °F (5,500 °C);
    • The corona - up to 3.5 million °F (2 million °C).
  5. Distance: 93 million miles (150 million kilometers) from the Earth.
  6. Orbit Duiration: 230 million years to complete an orbit around the Milky Way.
  7. Day Duration: The Sun is not solid and its parts rotate with different speed:
    • At the equator: 25 Earth days;
    • At the poles: 36 Earth days.
  8. Speed: 450,000 miles per hour (720,000 kilometers per hour).

Curious Facts:

  • Its gravity holds the Solar System together and keep everything in orbit around it.
  • It is the largest object in the Solar System, 100 times wider than the Earth.
  • Its geographic poles N and S change their polarity at almost every 11 years.
  • The corona is much hotter than the layer below it.
  • The alignment of the Sun, the Moon and the Earth results in eclipses
