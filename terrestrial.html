Terrestrial Planets

Mercury

Read time: 3 min

  1. Name Origine: The fastest of the ancient Roman gods.
  2. Size: 3,032 miles (4,879 kilometers)
  3. Type: Terrestrial
  4. Mean Temperature: 333°F (167°C)
  5. Distance: average distance of 36 million miles (58 million kilometers) from the Sun
  6. Orbit Duration: 88 Earth Days
  7. Day Duration: 59 Earth days
  8. Speed: 29.4 miles (47.4 kilometers) per second

Curious Facts:

  • It is the smallest planet in the Solar System.
  • It is the nearest to the Sun planet.
  • It is the fastest planet.
  • It is the second densest planet after the Earth: 85% of its radius is taken by a big metallic core.
  • It orbits almost upright around the Sun.

Venus

Read time: 3 min

  1. Name Origine: Ancient Roman goddess of Love and beauty.
  2. Size: 7,521 miles (12,104 kilometers)
  3. Type: Terrestrial
  4. Mean Temperature: 867°F (464°C)
  5. Distance: average distance of 67.2 million miles (108.2 million kilometers) from the Sun
  6. Orbit Duration: 224.7 Earth days
  7. Day Duration: 243 Earth days
  8. Speed: 21.8 miles (35.0 kilometers) per second

Curious Facts:

  • Venus spins slowly in the opposite direction from most planets.
  • It the hottest planet in our solar system because of its thick atmosphere.
  • It is the only planet named after a female god..
  • At Earth, Venus is seen to be the brightest object in the night sky after the Moon. They both have phases – full, half, quarter, etc. but Venus completes the cycle for 584 Earth days.
  • It has an induced magnetic field, not internally generated.

Earth

Read time: 3 min

  1. Name Origine: Germanic word, meaning “the ground.”
  2. Size: 7,926 miles (12,756 kilometers)
  3. Type: Terrestrial
  4. Mean Temperature: 59°F (15°C)
  5. Distance: 93 million miles (150 million kilometers).
  6. Orbit Duration: 365.25 Earth Days
  7. Day Duration: 23.9 hours
  8. Speed: 18.5 miles (29.8 kilometers) per second.

Curious Facts:

  • Earth is the biggest of the terrestrial planets and the fifth largest planet overall.
  • Earth is the only planet that has a single moon.
  • It the only planet in our solar system with liquid water on the surface.
  • Ihe only place we know of so far that’s inhabited by living things.
  • Magnetic polarity of Earth can reverse at about every 400,000 years on average without harming life on the planet.

Mars

Read time: 3 min

  1. Name Origine: Roman god of war because the planet looks red associated with blood.
  2. Size: 4,221 miles (6,792 kilometers)
  3. Type: Terrestrial
  4. Mean Temperature: - 85°F (-65°C)
  5. Distance: 142 million miles (228 million kilometers)
  6. Orbit Duration: 8687 Earth Days
  7. Day Duration: 24.6 hours
  8. Speed: 15.0 miles (24.1 kilometers) per second.

Curious Facts:

  • Mars has two small moons - Phobos and Deimos, looking like potatoes.
  • Mars is one of the easiest planets to spot in the night sky because of its red colour.
  • It is one of the most explored bodies in the Solar System.
  • Mars has the largest volcano in the Solar System, Olympus Mons - three times taller than Earth's Mt. Everest .
  • Mars has no global magnetic field today but parts of its southern hemisphere show traces of a magnetic field from 4 billion years ago.
