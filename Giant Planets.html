Giants

Jupiter

Read time: 3 min

  1. Name Origin: the king of the ancient Roman gods.
  2. Size: 88,846 miles (142,984 kilometers).
  3. Type: Gas Giant
  4. Mean Temperature: - 166°F (-110°C)
  5. Distance: 484 million miles (778 million kilometers)
  6. Orbit Duration: 11.86 Earth years (4,333 Earth days)
  7. Day Duration: 9.93 hours
  8. Speed: 8.1 miles (13.1 kilometers) per second.

Curious Facts:

  • It is the biggest planet in the Solar System.
  • Jupiter has the shortest day in the Solar System.
  • Its enormous magnetic field is 16 to 54 times as powerful as that of the Earth
  • Jupiter the largest ocean in the solar system – an ocean made of hydrogen instead of water.
  • Jupiter has 95 moons that are officially recognized and faint ring system.

Saturn

Read time: 3 min

  1. Name Origine: the Roman god of agriculture and wealth, and father of Jupiter.
  2. Size: 74,897 miles (120,536 kilometers).
  3. Type: Gas Giant
  4. Mean Temperature: Minus 220°F (-140°C)
  5. Distance: 886 million miles (1.4 billion kilometers)
  6. Orbit Duration: 29.4 Earth years
  7. Day Duration: 10.7 hours
  8. Speed: 6.0 miles (9.7 kilometers) per second

Curious Facts:

  • The farthest planet from Earth discovered by the unaided human eye.
  • As of June 8, 2023, Saturn has 146 officially confirmed moons in its orbit.
  • It has spectacular ring system where each ring orbits at a different speed around the planet.
  • Saturn's north pole has an interesting atmospheric feature – a six-sided jet stream.
  • Saturn is the only planet in our solar system with an average density that is less than water.

Uranus

Read time: 3 min

  1. Name Origin: the Greek god of the sky, the father of Kronos (or Saturn in Roman mythology).
  2. Size: 31,763 miles (51,118 kilometers)
  3. Type: Ice Giant
  4. Mean Temperature: - 320°F (-195°C)
  5. Distance: 1.8 billion miles (2.9 billion kilometers)
  6. Orbit Duration: 84 Earth years
  7. Day Duration: 17 hours and 14 minutes.
  8. Speed: 4.2 miles (6.8 kilometers) per second.

Curious Facts:

  • Uranus was the first planet found with the aid of a telescope.
  • It is surrounded by 13 faint rings and 27 small moons.
  • It is the only planet whose equator has a tilt of 97.77 degrees to its orbit and seems to rotate sideways.
  • Due to its unique tilt Uranus has the most extreme seasons in the solar system. The Sun shines directly over one of the poles for almost 21 Earth years, while the other half of the planet is covered by a dark winter.
  • It rotates in the opposite direction than most of the planets.

Neptune

Read time: 3 min

  1. Name Origin: Roman god of war because the planet looks red associated with blood.
  2. Size: 4,221 miles (6,792 kilometers)
  3. Type: Terrestrial
  4. Mean Temperature: - 85°F (-65°C)
  5. Distance: 142 million miles (228 million kilometers)
  6. Orbit Duration: 8687 Earth Days
  7. Day Duration: 24.6 hours
  8. Speed: 15.0 miles (24.1 kilometers) per second.

Curious Facts:

  • Neptune is the only planet in our solar system not visible to the naked eye.
  • It is the most distant planet in our solar system.
  • Neptune has 14 known moons.
  • Neptune has at least five main rings and four prominent ring arcs.
  • Neptune is our solar system's windiest world. The wind speed can reach more than 1,200 miles per hour (2,000 kilometers per hour).
  • Of the giant planets, Neptune is the densiest.
